## CYA-1 Internship Oppurtunity Exploration

This summer or in the following year, I plan to get an internship or career experience that will add more credibility to my resume and help me determine what niche of computer science I want to work and study in most. In the past, I have liked modifying operating systems or working with creative software but I am open to trying anything.

For my first CYA I reached out to employers, took advantage of WSU’s career resources, updated my resume, and managed to get an interview. This process has taken me many hours and will continue after this assignment is done and likely throughout the rest of my career. Getting my first interview was a milestone I reached, and although I didn’t land the role, my search continues. 

Preparation for internships has been ongoing throughout my studies, but as a Junior-level student, it is especially pertinent. In the spring, I got started by connecting with a handful of local employers at the career fair, though most simply routed me to online applications.

Next, I applied for a handful of computer science related roles, found through the career fair, handshake, indeed, and other online browsing. The following companies are just some that I have confirmed applications for, including: Idaho National Library, VICEROY, Conversion Logix, Garmin, Multnomah County, XPO and Avista Corporation, and Hill Airforce Base. 

I was made aware of the Hill Airforce Base software engineering role through my academic advisor. I attended virtual info sessions to become familiar with the program and optimize my chances of landing an internship. I tailored my CV to include relevant skills and after a few days, I received an interview invitation. 

The interview went quite well, and I got the impression that the interviewers were impressed with my GPA and current skillset. I was told I would receive an acceptance email if chosen. After a week elapsed, I reached out and it was confirmed that I was not chosen. My interviewer suggested that I reapply again and gave me her contact information. I believe I could have been more specific on my project details to score better.

For all other applications, I received 2 rejections and the remainder are in pending status. I intend on circling back to those that have not definitively rejected me, just to confirm I was not chosen and to show my continued interest. 

I am still actively pursuing internship and career opportunities and plan on using the career center to improve my interview skills as well as network to find more opportunities. I am proud of having done my first interview. Despite being rejected, I feel more comfortable reaching out with employers and advocating for my skills in interviews. I also have learned that the career field of computer science is in a tough spot right now, regardless I can continue applying myself and gaining skills that will make me a valuable candidate.
